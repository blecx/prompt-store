# Authoring Lifecycle

A stable technical-book workflow usually moves through these states.

## 1. Conception
You define the reader, scope, intent, and promise of the book.

## 2. Structure
You create the table of contents and establish chapter purposes.

## 3. Foundational drafting
You write the chapters that define the vocabulary and conceptual boundaries.

## 4. Expansion
You draft deeper architecture, engineering, evaluation, safety, and operations chapters.

## 5. Consolidation
You reduce overlap, improve transitions, and align terms across chapters.

## 6. Evidence hardening
You validate claims, improve references, and tighten examples.

## 7. Publication preparation
You complete front matter, references, appendices, glossary, legal details, and production notes.

## 8. Final quality control
You run a strict technical and editorial review.
