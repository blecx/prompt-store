# Quality Gates

Use these gates before you call a chapter “good enough”.

## Gate 1 — Structural completeness
- chapter purpose is clear
- audience fit is clear
- section hierarchy is coherent
- chapter belongs in the book

## Gate 2 — Terminology discipline
- key terms are defined
- adjacent concepts are separated clearly
- terminology is consistent with earlier chapters

## Gate 3 — Technical rigor
- mechanisms are explained
- trade-offs are explicit
- failure modes are included
- implementation boundaries are clear

## Gate 4 — Evidence and realism
- unsupported claims are marked
- references are logged
- speculative content is labeled
- operational constraints are acknowledged

## Gate 5 — Publication fitness
- markdown formatting is clean
- summary and takeaways exist
- glossary candidates are captured
- figure/table suggestions are identified
