# Deep-Research-to-Report Skill

Transform the provided **deep research output** into a **world-class professional report**.

The result must not remain a research memo. It must become a disciplined report that is useful for decisions, reviews, architecture discussions, procurement choices, or strategic direction.

## What to preserve

- substantive findings
- source-awareness
- nuance
- contradictions where they matter

## What to improve

- decision framing
- section logic
- synthesis across findings
- options and trade-offs
- actionable recommendations
- implementation implications
- risks and limitations

## Mandatory rules

- do not flatten nuance into simplistic conclusions
- do not hide evidence weaknesses
- do not invent certainty
- state unresolved questions explicitly
- include a meaningful executive summary
- use comparison tables if multiple options or categories exist

## Required structure

1. Executive Summary
2. Context, Objective, and Scope
3. Methodology and Evidence Basis
4. Key Findings
5. Analysis and Interpretation
6. Options and Trade-offs
7. Recommendations
8. Implementation Roadmap / Next Steps
9. Risks and Limitations
10. References / Placeholders
11. Appendices / Supporting Material Placeholders
